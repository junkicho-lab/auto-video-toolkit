## Scene 01 — Arena silhouette
There's this moment — where one guy has it all. The title, the fear, the whole damn world watching him.

## Scene 02 — Gloves on canvas
And then he just... lets it go. Not 'cause somebody took it. 'Cause he was already broken before the first bell ever rang.

## Scene 03 — Brownsville boy
Mike Tyson. Born June 30th, 1966. Brooklyn, New York. But he didn't grow up — he survived.

## Scene 04 — Police sirens
Brownsville was one of the roughest neighborhoods in America. No dad. Mom could barely keep the lights on. By eleven, he'd been picked up by cops over forty times.

## Scene 05 — Juvenile detention
At twelve, they shipped him upstate to Tryon — a reform school that was basically a prison for kids.

## Scene 06 — Cus and boy
That's where he met Cus D'Amato. An old-school boxing trainer who saw something in this angry kid nobody else could see.

## Scene 07 — Gym training
Cus didn't just teach him how to fight. He took him in. Became his legal guardian. Gave him something he'd never had — structure, discipline, somebody who actually gave a damn.

## Scene 08 — Fireside
Cus told him, straight up — "You're gonna be the youngest heavyweight champion in history." And Mike? He believed every single word.

## Scene 09 — Knockout punch
March 6th, 1985 — Mike goes pro. First fight, first-round knockout. Then he just kept doing it. Twenty-seven straight wins. Twenty-five by KO. Guys couldn't even last a minute.

## Scene 10 — Cus gravestone
But Cus never got to see any of it. He died on November 4th, 1985. Pneumonia. Mike was nineteen. The one person who kept him grounded — gone.

## Scene 11 — Referee count
One year later, Mike knocked out Trevor Berbick in two rounds. WBC heavyweight champion. Twenty years old. Youngest ever. Just like Cus said.

## Scene 12 — Champion belts
By '87, he had all three belts. WBC, WBA, IBF. Undisputed. Twenty-one years old. On top of the world — and completely alone.

## Scene 13 — Hotel solitude
Without Cus, there was nobody around to keep him in check. Promoter Don King slid right in. And the wrong crowd? They followed.

## Scene 14 — TV interview
In '88, he married Robin Givens. Eight months later, she goes on national TV — Mike sitting right there — and tells Barbara Walters the marriage is "pure hell."

## Scene 15 — Courthouse steps
The divorce was finalized on Valentine's Day, 1989. Cameras everywhere. Reporters screaming his name. You can't make that stuff up.

## Scene 16 — Tokyo defeat
Then came Tokyo. February 1990. Buster Douglas — a 42-to-1 underdog — knocked Mike out in the tenth. The baddest man on the planet, face down on the canvas, reaching for his mouthpiece.

## Scene 17 — Courtroom
It got worse. In '92, he was convicted of rape. Sentenced to six years. Served three in an Indiana prison.

## Scene 18 — Prison cell
Three years behind bars. No ring, no crowd, no belt. Just a concrete wall and time. A whole lot of time.

## Scene 19 — Bite incident
He came back in '95, won a few fights. But the rematch with Holyfield — June '97 — that's the one everybody remembers. Third round, Mike bit off a chunk of the man's ear. Spit it on the canvas. Then bit the other one.

## Scene 20 — Empty gym decline
After that, the magic was just... gone. He kept showing up, kept fighting, but everyone watching could tell — it wasn't the same guy anymore.

## Scene 21 — Locker room defeat
By 2003, the man who'd earned over four hundred million dollars filed for bankruptcy. Twenty-three million in debt.

## Scene 22 — Foreclosed mansion
Four hundred million dollars. Exposed mansions, exotic cars, all of it — gone. Just like that. Nothing left.

## Scene 23 — Broadway show
But here's what nobody saw coming. Mike Tyson found peace. Did a one-man show on Broadway. Showed up in The Hangover. Built a fifty-million-dollar weed empire.

## Scene 24 — Ranch peace
Somehow, after everything — the fights, the prison, the money, the chaos — the guy found a way to be okay.

## Scene 25 — Netflix fight
In 2024, at fifty-eight years old, he stepped back in the ring. Jake Paul. Netflix. A hundred and eight million people watching. He lost — but he walked out on his own two feet. Mike Tyson. Still standing.
